Simple Quadratic Derivative Application

Tracking the daily growth speed (biomass accumulation) of Malaysian Lowland Cabbage using a standard high-school level polynomial equation.

🌱 The Growth Problem

In food systems optimization, we need to know exactly when a crop's daily growth speed starts stalling. This helps smart greenhouses budget fertilizer delivery and predict optimal harvest days.

🧮 Clean Math Formulas

Cabbage Weight: $f(x)$
$f(x) = 6x - 0.05x^2$
$x$ = Days (0 to 60) | $f(x)$ = Weight (Grams)
Growth Speed: $f'(x)$
$f'(x) = 6 - 0.1x$
Calculated via basic Power Rule. Measures Grams/Day.

Growth window curves adapted to generalized trends from MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) crop data manuals.
